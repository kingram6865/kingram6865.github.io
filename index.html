Hello, I'm Ken Ingram!

I became fascinated with all types of technology at a young age, and computers have been the at the top of my list of interests. I have a background in science, with a degree in Applied Mathematics, focusing on Statistics. I worked for a time in operations in a Site Reliability Engineering role.

I enjoy learning new technologies that can be used in the field of computer science and software engineering, and determining ways to use these technologies to solve problems. These days I am most curious about AI and Blockchain, and developing APIs, Microservices, infrastructure and front ends for applications.

I became an independent contractor so that I could take more time to study full-time with my Eskrima teacher. I am a lineage holder in the Filipino martial art Visayan Style Corto Kadena Eskrima, a system designed, developed and cultivated by my late teacher Grandmaster Santiago "Sonny" Umpad. I also enjoy spending time as a sous chef of gourmet dinners for groups of 20-25 people bi-monthly for the last 10 years. In my remaining spare time my avocation is legal research to understand the history of the legal process behind American government and American society.

Job-Search Progress-Tracker

Job-Search Progress-Tracker is a full stack web app for users to save and track their job search history, including actions taken related to any one job application. After signing in, users can access all companies and jobs that have been added to the datastore, including those added by other users, and have a custom list of actions related to a job being tracked. The App uses a custom API from a backend constructed using Ruby, Rails and PostgreSQL. The front end is built using React, implementing Axios for HTTP requests to the custom API.
